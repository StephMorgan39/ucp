The Product Data Journey

Every time you upload a supplier's product list, it goes through this process:

1

📥 Staging (You upload)

Your supplier list arrives and sits in a waiting area called "Staging". Each product is assigned an ID for tracking.

↓
2

🔍 Detection (System checks)

The system looks for any suspicious or unusual data — e.g., duplicate products, invalid prices, missing information.

↓
3

⚠️ Validation Gate (System decides)

If something looks wrong, the product is flagged and HELD for your review before it can continue.

↓
4

👁️ Nina's Review (You approve)

You check the flagged products, decide if they're OK, and give the thumbs-up (or fix the issue)

↓
5

📦 Master Data (System routes)

Approved products flow into the Master Database where they're organized by type (stock, pricing, specs, etc.)

↓
6

🎯 Live System (Ready to use)

Products are now live and available to be seen on your website, used in specs, or exported to partners.

💡 Why multiple stages? This careful process prevents bad data from reaching customers. Each quality gate catches issues early, letting you fix them before they cause problems.

ETL Terminology Made Simple

Don't know what these terms mean? You're not alone. Here are the concepts in plain English:

📥 Staging

A temporary holding area where supplier data waits before processing. Think of it like the "inbox" of your product system.

🔄 ETL

Extract-Transform-Load. Fancy way of saying: grab data from a supplier, clean/standardize it, then move it into your system.

📦 Master Data

The "official" version of each product. It combines info from all suppliers and is the single source of truth.

⚠️ Anomaly

Something the system detected that looks weird or wrong. Could be a typo, a suspiciously high price, or a product that looks like a duplicate.

🔐 Validation Gate

A quality checkpoint. The system verifies data before letting it pass through to the next stage.

🎯 SKU

Stock Keeping Unit. A unique code assigned to each product for tracking and ordering.

💾 Sync

When data updates flow between systems automatically. Like your phone syncing with the cloud.

🏷️ Standardization

Converting different words/formats into one standard. E.g., "Tile Gloss" + "Gloss Tile" + "Glossy" all become "Gloss".

✨ You don't need to memorize these! Use this reference whenever you see a technical term and wonder what it means.
